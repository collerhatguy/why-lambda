One of my main reasons for wanting to push through lambda academy is monetary profit. Ever since I was young I had a propensity to collect small change in jars and count my savings on a regular basis. Now that I’m an adult living on my own dollar my obsession with money is even stronger. I hate worrying about whether I can afford rent for x time and want to get to the point where I don't ever think about money. I would also like to be able to have kids down the line at some point and so money is necessary for that too.

Another reason for going to lambda is that I genuinely like coding. It's very satisfying to have a brain child come into your head and putting forth the effort to bring it into reality. I had been trying to learn web development for about a year and didn’t get any job offers, but I don't regret any of it because I built so much and learned so much.

Making friends is another motivation for attending. It's very rare for me to meet someone who understands and is interested in what I'm working on. So, I'm excited at the idea of being able to meet like minded people and see what they produce and vice versa. There were alot of points where I was really stuck on a project and thought it would be the best thing if I could have a second pair of eyes looking at my screen. It will also be satisfying to help other people with problems that I faced when I first started out.

Finally going to lambda is an opportunity for me to become more disciplined. For a good portion of my life I have struggled to stay focused on any one thing and commit to certain goals. Doing something difficult and rewarding like lambda is an opportunity to become sharper and more focused as a person as well as becoming more skilled. I want to develop my character along with my skills.
